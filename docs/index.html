Bitcoin Approaches the $100K Threshold as Institutional Inflows Reach Record Levels

By the AI Intelligence Desk — 14 min read

Across the global digital asset markets, a quiet conviction has taken hold. Bitcoin, the original cryptocurrency, is now within striking distance of the psychologically significant $100,000 mark — a level that, just three years ago, many analysts dismissed as speculative fantasy. The AI Advisor's proprietary models now assign a 73% probability to a sustained break above this threshold within the next 21 trading days.

The driving forces are not singular but structural. Spot ETF inflows have averaged $847 million daily over the past month, dwarfing the $312 million daily average seen during the initial ETF approval euphoria of early 2024. Corporate treasury allocations continue to accelerate, with seventeen publicly traded companies adding Bitcoin to their balance sheets in Q1 alone.

"The market has shifted from speculative enthusiasm to structural demand. This is no longer about narratives — it's about allocation frameworks."

Perhaps more telling is the behavior of long-term holders. On-chain data reveals that wallets holding Bitcoin for more than two years now control 71% of the circulating supply, the highest proportion since records began. This supply squeeze, combined with persistent demand, creates the conditions for asymmetric price movement that our models have been flagging since December.

Ethereum, meanwhile, presents a more nuanced picture. The successful implementation of proto-danksharding has reduced Layer 2 transaction costs by an order of magnitude, driving a resurgence in decentralized application activity. Total value locked across the Ethereum ecosystem has climbed to $148 billion, surpassing its previous all-time high.
